Introduction

As a student at a university where the location of buildings are separated by a combination of uphills and downhills, riding a board is not really an all-around solution to get from place to place.

With the growing trend of utilizing versatile and cost-effective means of electric transportation for short distance commutes, an electric longboard would be a very cool choice for someone who normally rides a board. As an engineering student who enjoys understanding the inner-workings of the things that I use, building one not only will result a unique solution but the experience that can be obtained from doing so would be invaluable.

With advancements in brushless motor and battery technology and their continuing popularity on the end consumer, there are a variety of components to choose from for this project build at a reduced cost.

Focusing on motors and the inherent benefits of a brushless motor compared to its brushed counterpart, there is a much higher power-to-weight ratio thus providing the end user more flexibility in leveraging the requirements of their system. As a result, a brushless motor will be used for the electic longboard.

Current battery technology has also made strides over the past few years in particular lithium-based batteries. Lithium batteries have a much higher energy capacity and can last for longer durations without the effects of long-term fluctuations in the output. As a result, lithium batteries are an ideal candidate for an electric longboard project.

The Hand Controller

The goal of the hand controller is to provide an interface to the electric longboard. This would include controlling the different aspects of the board and possibly relaying information and statistics of the whole system.

This hand controller will house all of the necessary electronics and will possibly be 3D-printed as a robust and cost-effective approach.
